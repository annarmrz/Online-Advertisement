The 17 Sustainable Development Goals:
A Guide for Better Future

Goal 1

Aims to eliminate extreme poverty and relative poverty based on national definitions. The impoverished are vulnerable to economic and political crises, biodiversity loss, natural disasters, and violence.

Goal 2

The 2030 Agenda aims to abolish hunger and malnutrition in 15 years. Due to rising global demand, food production must double by 2050.

Goal 3

maintains the MDGs' focus on child and maternal mortality, infectious diseases like AIDS, malaria, and tuberculosis, and non-communicable diseases including diabetes, traffic accidents, and narcotics misuse.

Goal 4

ensures that all children, adolescents, and adults, especially the most marginalized and disadvantaged, have educational and training opportunities.

Goal 5

promotes equal economic opportunities for men and women, the eradication of violence against women and girls, and early and forced marriage.

Goal 6

strives to improve water quality and prevent chemical-related pollution. Cross-border collaboration is also recommended for managing water resources at all levels.

Goal 7

aims to raise the share of renewables in the global energy mix and double the worldwide pace of energy efficiency improvement since sustainable development depends on climate-friendly economic growth.

Goal 8

focuses on economic growth, productivity, and jobs. It aims to eradicate modern slavery and human trafficking by 2030.

Goal 9

intends to assist technology development, research, and innovation, especially in developing nations, and to increase small-scale industrial and other enterprises' integration into value chains and markets.

Goal 10

aims to provide equal opportunities by eliminating discriminatory laws, rules, and practices and by implementing appropriate migration policies.

Goal 11

reduces cities' per capita environmental effect, especially in air quality and garbage management.

Goal 12

implements the UN's 10-year framework of sustainable consumption and production programs. It promotes environmentally sound management of chemicals and garbage, as well as recycling to reduce waste.

Goal 13

asks countries to include climate protection in their national policies and help each other meet obstacles.

Goal 14

proposes eliminating marine pollution and ocean acidification by 2025 and safeguarding marine and coastal ecosystems by 2020.

Goal 15

aims to stop deforestation, restore degraded forests, and increase reforestation by 2020.

Goal 16

for 2030 consequently attempts to foster peaceful and inclusive societies. It emphasizes minimizing violence, abolishing torture, and fighting organized crime.

Goal 17

asks rich countries to renew their pledge to donate 0.7% of their GDP to aid.
